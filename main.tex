\ctparttext{
In the following part, we introduce several novel approaches tackling the \textit{Semantic Gap}.
First, we present a pattern-based algorithm for the annotation of type information from unstructured text.
Second, we describe AGDISTIS -- a knowledge base agnostic state of the art system for named entity disambiguation. 
Finally, we explain our approach towards web-scale extraction of RDF knowledge from strongly templated websites.  
}
\part{Semantic Gap}
\input{part_02/unstructured_annotation/chapter_313.tex} %type annotation
\input{part_02/unstructured_annotation/chapter_311.tex} %Agdistis
\input{part_02/semi_structured_annotation/ISWC_REX/341_rex.tex}


\cleardoublepage
\ctparttext{
The last part explained, how to extract high-quality semantic data from unstructured and semi-structured data sources with scalable algorithms.
However, the multitude of diverse algorithms, especially in the area of semantic annotation of unstructured text, lead to a lack of easily deployable datasets as well as a lack of experiment comparability, repeatability  and archivability.
To close the \textit{Evaluation Gap}, we present an interchangable dataset for \ac{NER} and \ac{NED} as well as an open science-enabling benchmarking platform for semantic annotation approaches.
}
\part{Evaluation Gap}
\input{part_02/benchmarking/LREC_N3NIFNERNED/chapter_331.tex}

In this part of the thesis, we introduce HAWK, a novel approach for hybrid question answering. 
With this approach, we aim at closing the \textit{Information Gap}. 
HAWK leverages the access to unstructured as well as structured knowledge for end-users and non-experts.
}
